# WeGro Business Model

[[WeGro]] operates across four interconnected business lines in Bangladesh agriculture.

## 1. Inputs Business

### Seeds
- White-labelled seeds (60-80% margins)
- Exclusive distributorship of global brands (40-50% margins)
- Market size: ~$2B in Bangladesh, highly fragmented
- Partners: Syngenta, Lalteer, BRAC Seed
- Opportunity: Bangladesh lacks local seed R&D; WeGro can be the exclusive distribution layer for global companies

### Fertilisers
- Chemical fertilisers — differentiated by efficiency, developing liquid variants to reduce runoff
- Biofertilisers — WeGro's own line, operates a small compost factory
- Coco peat — ~10x better than soil when combined with biofertilisers

### Micronutrients
- Sulphur, magnesium, zinc (chemical and biological sources)
- ~5-10 kg per hectare

### Machines
- **Multi-crop dryers** — biggest opportunity. Farmers lose value from slow sun-drying
- Drones — planned for launch
- Tillers — evaluated but deprioritized

### Land & Greenhouses
- Seedling business — better yield vs direct seeding, requires germination rooms
- Net houses — India model: government-subsidized, pesticide-free, GAP-certified crops exportable to US/EU

## 2. Financing Business

### Crop Collateralised Financing
- Farmers store crops in partner warehouses, get loans against crop as collateral
- Electronic Warehouse Receipt (EWR) system for traceability

### Trade Financing (Factoring)
- Factories with capacity but limited working capital get financing against confirmed buyer orders
- WeGro facilitates — doesn't hold inventory

### Commodity Trading
- Price prediction, stockpiling for better prices
- Futures-style contracts with expiry dates

## 3. Trading / Output Business
- Aggregation: combine smallholder output to fill large buyer orders (e.g., 100 tons from many 20-ton farmers)
- Contract growing: take work orders from large companies, source/grow product
- Stockpile for better pricing using commodity models

## 4. Data Business
- **Customers**: Banks and financial institutions
- **Context**: Bangladesh central bank mandates $3.5B/year in agricultural lending; banks generate NPLs because they lend to large factories instead of farmers
- **WeGro's solution**:
  - Geo-tagged land photos (tamper-proof via app)
  - Satellite imagery (free tier) for 7-day crop monitoring
  - AI-based yield prediction
  - Non-monetary farmer credit scoring
  - Buyer-pays-farmer model eliminates collections problem
- **Revenue**: ~$10/farmer; banks generate ~5x return through better lending
- **TAM**: $20B agricultural financing market in Bangladesh

## Risk Management
- Direct input distribution (no cash handling)
- Frequent field officer oversight
- Multiple cultivation cycles reduce seasonal risk
- Strong buyer networks for timely settlement
- Digital monitoring for transparency

## Key Bottlenecks
- Founder acknowledges spreading too thin — experimenting across many initiatives to find top 2-3
- Board feedback: "Team can't keep up"
- Strength is commercial/business side; needs agronomist co-founder for technical depth
